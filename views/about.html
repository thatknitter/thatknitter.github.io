About Me

At the end of my life I do not want to be that person who looks out over their empire, knowing I'd made a lot of money. I want my work to impact lives. I want to build things that empower people and assist in social change. I want my community to be enriched because I wrote code.

I'm a programmer and knitter who is currently based out of Nashville, TN. I started teaching myself to code in 2013, and in the fall of 2014 I started my apprenticeship at Nashville Software School. I started work on "The Athletic Knitter," a book of knitting patterns based around athleticware in January of 2015, and I anticipate publishing the e-book to Ravelry by the end of 2016.

I'm a science and technology enthusiast with a passion for computers. I started teaching myself how to build computers and circuit boards in the fall of 2014, and have recently started working with Arduino Microcontrollers. I want to share this passion and knowledge with the world, and have started volunteering with the Nashville Public Library to teach programming to kids in a fun way that plays to their inherent interests.
